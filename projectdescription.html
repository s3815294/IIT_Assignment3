Project Description

Team Project Description

Overview

Topic

Recycling is a conscious act of generosity for consumers that is very beneficial for the community and environment but actually determining what items are recyclable and what are non-recyclable is such a hassle that the majority of consumers would simply dispose these items into landfill waste which would take up more space in landfill waste and increase the impacts of landfill waste.

Our topic is to develop an app on mobile devices that allows quick identification of daily products and items that can be recyclable which would reduce the threshold for classifying waste resulting in greater recycling rates. With increasing recycling rates, it can also potentially promote the recycling industry and reduce the impacts of waste on the environment.

Motivation

In 2018-19, Australia had generated up to 2.5 megatonnes (DAWE 2020:XI) where only 15% of plastics were recycled (DAWE 2020:XII). During the same time, Australia had exported a total of 187 kilotonnes of plastic and had reused 393 kilotonnes of plastic (DAWE 2020:19). The report provided by the Department of Agriculture, Water and Environment from the government shows that Australia urgently needs to improve in recycling plastics and any products made of plastic that exist in large quantities in our daily life. Knowing this fact, it became our motivation for this project.

In our project, we will be utilising artificial technology that is present in the modern IT development trend along with barcode recognition technology to provide users with quick item recycling guides.

This project has broad prospects in the future. It can help people more easily to identify materials that can be recycled, and help improve consumers' recycling rate of recyclable products in their daily lives. In the future, it may also promote the manufacturer puts a mark on the recyclable material that can be used for software identification, and if enough users use it, it can add a recommendation for the corresponding product that uses the recycled material, which will improve the utilisation rate of the recycled material and generate commercial value.

Landscape

In the mobile app market a few apps with the same purpose are already available such as My Waste, Recycle Smart or Brisbane Bin and Recycling. Although having a vast competition, there are a couple features that these apps lack such as being restricted to a certain region, having to pay to utilise the app or have very limited functionality. Our app aims to cover a large area so consumers around the world, starting with Australia, would be able to use the app for free with new features that are not available in other apps such as our AI detector which works by just clicking a photo of a product and automatically being informed whether the product is recyclable or not along with this feature, our app aims to also utilise the in-built GPS in the mobile device so that it can locate the nearest recycling bin if the user does not have one in their home.
